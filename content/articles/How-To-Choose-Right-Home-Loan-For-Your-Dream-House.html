🏠 Types of Home Loans in India

Before comparing lenders, you need to understand what kind of loan suits your situation. Most borrowers default to a standard home purchase loan — but depending on your need, a different product may serve you better.

🔒

Fixed Rate Home Loan

Your interest rate stays the same throughout the loan tenure, regardless of RBI repo rate changes.

✔ EMI never changes — easy to budget
✔ Protected from rate hikes
✘ Higher rate than floating at entry
✘ No benefit when rates fall
📈

Floating Rate Home Loan

Rate is linked to RBI repo rate via EBLR. EMI moves up or down with market conditions.

✔ Lower starting rate vs fixed
✔ Benefits when RBI cuts rates
✘ EMI uncertainty during rate hike cycles
✘ Long-term budgeting is harder
🔄

Hybrid / Semi-Fixed Loan

Fixed rate for an initial period (2–5 years), then switches to floating. Combines stability and flexibility.

✔ EMI stability in early years
✔ Floating benefit in later tenure
✘ Rate at switch may not be favourable
✘ Less common — fewer lenders offer this
🏗️

Home Construction Loan

Disbursed in stages linked to construction milestones. Interest is charged only on the disbursed amount.

✔ Interest only on amount used
✔ Suited for plot + construction
✘ More documentation required
✘ Disbursement tied to builder milestones
💡 Which Type Should You Choose? In a falling interest rate environment — like when the RBI is in a rate-cutting cycle — a floating rate loan is almost always the better choice. In a rising rate cycle or if you want complete EMI predictability, a fixed rate gives peace of mind. For most Indian borrowers in 2026, floating rate linked to EBLR is the dominant and recommended choice.

✅ Home Loan Eligibility — What Lenders Look At

Your eligibility determines not just whether you get the loan, but how much you get and at what rate. Lenders evaluate multiple parameters simultaneously — improving even one or two of these can significantly improve your loan offer.

Parameter Ideal Profile Impact on Loan Status
CIBIL Score 750 and above Lower rate, faster approval, higher amount Critical
Income Stable salary or 3+ years ITR (self-employed) Determines maximum loan amount (typically 5–6x annual income) Critical
Age Below 45 for 20-year tenure Older applicants get shorter tenures, higher EMI Important
Existing EMIs / FOIR Total EMIs below 40–50% of income High FOIR reduces eligible amount Important
Employment Type Salaried in private/govt sector Self-employed needs higher documentation Moderate
Property Type Approved residential project Unapproved plots or rural land face restrictions Check
Co-applicant Working spouse or parent Boosts eligibility, also unlocks additional tax benefit Recommended

⚠️ Hidden Charges Nobody Tells You About

The advertised interest rate is rarely the true cost of your home loan. Lenders layer multiple fees and charges that can add ₹50,000 to ₹2 lakh or more to your total outgo. Always ask for a complete fee schedule before signing.

🔍 Hidden & Overlooked Charges — Full List

!
Processing Fee — 0.25% to 1% of the loan amount. On a ₹50L loan, that's ₹12,500 to ₹50,000. Some lenders waive this during festive offers — always negotiate.
!
Legal & Technical Charges — For property document verification and valuation. ₹5,000 to ₹15,000. Usually non-refundable even if loan is rejected.
▲
Prepayment / Foreclosure Charges — Floating rate loans: NIL (RBI mandate). Fixed rate loans: up to 2–3% of outstanding principal. Huge impact if you plan to repay early.
▲
Conversion / Switching Fee — When you shift from fixed to floating or vice versa, or when you switch lenders (balance transfer). Can be 0.5% to 1% of outstanding loan.
▲
MODT Charges (Memorandum of Deposit of Title Deed) — Stamp duty charged by state government to register the mortgage. Ranges from 0.1% to 0.5% of loan amount depending on state.
✔
Insurance Premium — Some lenders bundle home loan insurance (PMI or credit life). This is optional — do not accept it as mandatory. Evaluate independently.
✔
CERSAI Registration Fee — ₹50 to ₹500. Mandatory central registry for mortgage security. Small but should appear transparently in your cost sheet.

📋 Step-by-Step: How to Apply for a Home Loan

1

Check & Improve Your CIBIL Score

Get your free credit report from CIBIL, Experian, or CRIF. Resolve any errors, clear outstanding debts, and avoid new credit enquiries for at least 3–6 months before applying.

2

Calculate Your Eligibility & Budget

Use online EMI calculators to understand how much you can borrow based on your income and existing obligations. Keep your total EMI outgo below 40% of take-home salary.

3

Compare At Least 3–5 Lenders

Don't just compare interest rates — compare processing fees, prepayment terms, customer service, and disbursal timelines. Use aggregator platforms like BankBazaar or PaisaBazaar for side-by-side comparison.

4

Get Pre-Approval / In-Principle Sanction

Apply for a pre-approval letter before finalising the property. This tells the builder or seller you are a serious buyer and gives you negotiating power on the property price.

5

Submit Documents & Await Sanction

Typical documents: KYC (Aadhaar, PAN), income proof (salary slips, ITR), bank statements (6 months), property documents. Keep originals and attested copies ready.

6

Read the Sanction Letter Carefully

The sanction letter has the final rate, tenure, EMI, and all charges. Read every clause. Check the spread above EBLR, reset frequency, and prepayment conditions before signing.

7

Register Mortgage & Collect Documents

After disbursal, the lender registers the mortgage and holds the original property documents. Get a certified copy of all documents and keep the acknowledgement of original document custody safely.

🧾 Tax Benefits on Home Loans

A home loan is one of the most tax-efficient financial products available to Indian taxpayers under the old tax regime. These deductions can reduce your annual tax outgo by ₹1 lakh to ₹2.5 lakh depending on your income slab.

Section Benefit Maximum Deduction Condition
Section 24(b) Interest paid on home loan ₹2,00,000 p.a. Self-occupied property. No limit for let-out.
Section 80C Principal repayment ₹1,50,000 p.a. Combined limit with other 80C investments
Section 80EE Additional interest deduction ₹50,000 p.a. First-time buyer, loan ≤ ₹35L, property ≤ ₹50L
Section 80EEA Affordable housing interest ₹1,50,000 p.a. Stamp duty value ≤ ₹45L, first-time buyer
💰 Maximum Annual Tax Saving Possible If you qualify for Section 24(b) + 80C + 80EEA, the combined deduction can be up to ₹5,00,000 per year. For someone in the 30% tax bracket, that translates to a tax saving of approximately ₹1,50,000 per year. These benefits are available only under the old tax regime — if you have opted for the new regime, these deductions are not applicable.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What CIBIL score do I need for a home loan?

Most lenders require a minimum CIBIL score of 650–675 to sanction a home loan. However, to get the best available interest rates, you should target a score of 750 or above. Below 700, you may face higher rates, lower loan amounts, or outright rejection from top-tier banks. PSU banks like SBI tend to be slightly more flexible on credit score than private banks.

Fixed rate or floating rate — which is better in 2026?

In 2026, with the RBI in or approaching a rate-cutting cycle, a floating rate loan is generally the better choice. Floating rates — now linked to the External Benchmark Lending Rate (EBLR) — pass on RBI rate cuts directly to borrowers, which reduces your EMI or tenure automatically. Fixed rates make sense only if you expect rates to rise sharply or you want absolute EMI certainty for personal budgeting reasons.

Can I take a joint home loan and what are the benefits?

Yes — a joint home loan with a working spouse, parent, or sibling significantly boosts your eligibility amount. It also unlocks individual tax deductions for each co-applicant under Section 24(b) and 80C, effectively doubling the household tax benefit. If your spouse is a woman, many lenders also offer a 0.05% to 0.10% interest rate concession on joint loans where the woman is the primary applicant.

Should I make prepayments on my home loan?

For floating rate loans — absolutely yes, whenever you have surplus funds. Prepayments reduce the outstanding principal, which dramatically cuts the total interest payable over the loan life. Even a single annual prepayment of ₹50,000–₹1 lakh can reduce a 20-year loan by 2–4 years. There are no prepayment charges on floating rate home loans from banks (RBI mandated since 2012). For fixed rate loans, check the prepayment clause before committing.

What is a balance transfer and when should I consider it?

A home loan balance transfer means shifting your outstanding loan from your current lender to another lender offering a lower interest rate. It makes sense when the new rate is at least 0.50% lower than your current rate and you have a significant outstanding tenure remaining (at least 7–10 years). Factor in the processing fee, MODT charges, and switching costs before deciding — these can offset the savings if you're in the final years of repayment.

📌 Final Checklist Before You Sign

Before you put your signature on a home loan agreement, run through this final checklist. These are the questions that can save you lakhs over the life of your loan.

✅ Pre-Signing Checklist

✔
Have I compared at least 3 lenders on both rate AND total charges?
✔
Is the rate EBLR-linked (floating) or truly fixed — and do I know the spread above EBLR?
✔
Have I checked the prepayment and foreclosure clause specifically?
✔
Is the insurance bundled into the loan — and have I evaluated it independently?
▲
Is the total EMI outgo (all loans combined) below 40–45% of my take-home salary?
▲
Have I calculated the total interest outgo over the full tenure — not just the monthly EMI?
▲
Is the property legally clear — approved by local authority, RERA registered (if applicable)?
!
Am I aware of all the fees in the sanction letter — processing, legal, technical, MODT, CERSAI?
!
Have I read the rate reset clause — how frequently will my floating rate be reviewed and adjusted?

A home loan is a 20–30 year commitment. Take the time to choose it with the same care you took to choose the house itself. The right loan, chosen wisely, can save you more than the cost of a car. The wrong one, accepted in haste, can cost you decades of financial stress.
